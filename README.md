# 🌍 Global Inflation Analysis (1960–2024)

## 📖 Overview

This project provides a comprehensive analysis of historical inflation trends across **Peru**, **Latin America**, and **major global economies** from **1960 to 2024**. Using Python-based data analytics and visualization techniques, the study explores inflation patterns, economic crises, and long-term macroeconomic behavior through time-series analysis.

The project aims to identify key inflationary periods, compare regional and global trends, and provide data-driven insights into economic stability and volatility over time.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Analyze historical inflation trends in Peru.
- Compare Peru's inflation performance against global averages.
- Explore inflation dynamics across Latin American countries.
- Identify countries with the highest historical inflation rates.
- Visualize inflation patterns through different analytical approaches.
- Highlight major economic events such as hyperinflation periods.

## 📊 Analysis Performed

### 🇵🇪 Historical Inflation in Peru
Analysis of inflation evolution in Peru, including the hyperinflation crisis of the late 1980s and the stabilization period that followed.

### 🌎 Peru vs Global Inflation
Comparison between Peru’s inflation rate and the average inflation rate of the world's economies.

### 🇱🇦 Latin America Inflation Comparison
Comparative analysis of inflation trends among major Latin American countries such as Peru, Argentina, Brazil, Chile, Colombia, and Mexico.

### 📈 Top 10 Countries with Highest Historical Inflation
Ranking of countries based on their historical average inflation rates.

### 🔥 Inflation Heatmap by Decade
Visualization of inflation patterns across countries and decades to identify periods of economic instability.

## 🔍 Key Insights

- Peru experienced severe hyperinflation during the late 1980s and early 1990s.
- Latin American economies historically show higher inflation volatility compared to developed nations.
- Inflation trends have generally stabilized across most regions since the early 2000s.
- Major global economic crises are reflected in inflation fluctuations across multiple countries.
